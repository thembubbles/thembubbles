Current projects

PhD research (2018-2022)
Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Philippe Muchez
Co-supervisor: Dr. Niels Hulsbosch
In this project we are studying the phosphatic nodules from the Buranga pegmatite (western Rwanda, central Africa). These nodules usually occur on (and around) the contact between the intermediate and core zones of the dike, thus they are formed at the last stages of crystallization near the magmatic-hydrothermal transition. Additionally, phosphate minerals in these nodules are intensively altered by the interaction with a fluid phase. This alteration processes is commonly observed in many pegmatites and phosphate minerals around the world. However, the nature of the fluids in not constrained. Therefore, we want to characterize the textural and chemical evolution of both phosphate minerals and fluid inclusions present inside them to understand 1) how does the mineral and fluid composition change during crystallization; 2) what is the effect of fluid-melt-crystal interactions in the formation of pegmatites; 3) what processes dominate during the magmatic-hydrothermal transition; 4) the role of fluids in the mineralization of Li-Nb-Ta-Sn-W.
