FERNANDO PRADO ARAUJO

Brazilian | Geologist | PhD candidate @ KULeuven
Interested in Mineralogy and Petrology, Raman Spectroscopy, and Economic Geology

RESEARCH INTERESTS

Hi, I'm Fernando. I'm in the fourth and final year of my pursuit to obtain a PhD degree at KU Leuven, Belgium.

My research focuses on the formation and evolution of granitic melts (silica-rich), especially the processes occurring at the magmatic-hydrothermal transition and related with the mineralization of critical metals (Li, Ta, Nb, Sn, and W). I use a multi-technique approach, combining textural and geochemical micro-analysis of minerals and fluids to understand the 4D evolution of natural systems.

In my PhD project, I'm studying the Buranga pegmatite, a phosphorus-rich rare-element pegmatite (LCT family). The dike is quite famous by its outstanding phosphate mineralogy. The research looks into the fluid-melt-crystal interactions recorded by phosphate minerals and fluid inclusions within them to try to understand what's happened during the last stages of crystallization at the magmatic-hydrothermal transition. This study is also combined with the development and application of high-resolution Raman images at the state-of-the-art Raman microscopy lab at KULeuven.

Besides my work, I am also interested in nature in general and music. I really enjoy hiking and walking and I (try to) play guitar. I'm a devoted plant-carer, but not a very successful one (had many casualties in the mean time).
