Brazilian | Geologist | PhD candidate @ KULeuven
Mineralogy and Petrology, Raman spectroscopy, Economic Geology

RESEARCH INTERESTS

Hi, I'm Fernando. I'm in my fourth and final year of the PhD at KU Leuven, Belgium.

My research focusses on the petrology of granitic melts, especially the processes occurring at the magmatic-hydrothermal transition and related with the mineralization of critical metals (Li, Ta, Nb, Sn, and W). I use a multi-technique approach, combining textural and geochemical micro-analysis of minerals and fluids to understand the evolution of natural systems.

My PhD projects focus on using high temperature high pressure facilities to understand the differentiation and thermal structure of large igneous provinces (LIPs) and 3D textural analysis of minerals to reveal dynamic magmatic processes.

Besides my work, I am also interested in nature in general and music. I really enjoy hiking and walking and I (try to) play guitar. I'm a devoted plant carer, but not a very succesful one (had many casualties in the mean time).
